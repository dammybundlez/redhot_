film synopsis

Mother’s Love is a powerful coming-of-age story inspired by the life of iconic Nigerian actress and humanitarian Omotola Jalade-Ekeinde. At the heart of the film is a young woman’s journey from comfort to self-discovery, set against the backdrop of Nigeria’s National Youth Service Corps (NYSC) program. As she ventures into unfamiliar territory geographically, socially and emotionally, she begins to understand her mother's sacrifices, the true meaning of service and the value of her own voice. The film offers a rich blend of emotional storytelling, cultural authenticity and personal transformation. With themes of female empowerment, mother–daughter bonds, self-discovery and social responsibility, Mother’s Love is both deeply Nigerian and universally relatable.

Omotola Jalade-
Ekeinde

Director’s statement

Mother’s Love is more than a film to me, it’s a deeply personal journey. As my directorial debut, it draws inspiration from real experiences, not just my own, but from the many untold stories of young Nigerian women finding their place in a complex world. I wanted to tell a coming-of-age story that challenges privilege, confronts identity and ultimately celebrates the quiet strength of women redefining themselves on their own terms. Set against the vibrant, layered backdrop of modern Nigeria, Mother’s Love captures both the beauty and the friction of growth, how we wrestle with where we come from in order to discover who we truly are. This film is performance-led, emotionally grounded, and socially aware. It speaks to universal themes of family, resilience, and empowerment while offering a lens into contemporary Nigerian life rarely seen on screen. I believe authentic and bold stories like this deserve a global platform, and I’m honoured to share it with audiences ready to embrace something both intimate and impactful

– Omotola Jalade-Ekeinde
 Co-Writer & Director, Producer, Mother’s Love

Co Producer’s
Statement

Mother’s Love is a heartfelt exploration of the strength, sacrifice and silent resilience that define motherhood. Co-producing this film alongside Omotola Jalade Ekeinde was a powerful experience, as we sought to tell a story that honours the everyday heroism of women who love fiercely, even in the face of multiple challenges. This film is a tribute to the mothers whose stories often go untold. With sincerity and purpose, we aimed to create a narrative that not only moves audiences but also sparks meaningful reflection on the depth of a mother’s love. We are proud to share this story with the world.

By Tyna Obahiagbon
 Co-producer

Omotola Jalade Ekeinde is a renowned Nigerian actress, singer, philanthropist, and former model. She has starred in numerous Nollywood films and is celebrated for her compelling performances. Omotola is also recognised for her advocacy work on social issues and has received several awards for her contributions to the entertainment industry and humanitarian efforts. Her diverse talents and impactful presence make her a standout figure in African cinema and beyond.
Olumide Oworu
-AS: OBARO
@ olumideoworu
467k followers
Olumide Oworu is a versatile Nigerian actor, model, rapper and emerging politician. He began his acting career at the age of six with the television series Everyday People and gained prominence for his role as Tari in the Africa Magic series The Johnsons. He further showcased his talent as Weki in MTV Base's Shuga and as Ejiro in Netflix's Far From Home. His compelling performances have earned him accolades such as the "Nollywood Revelation of the Year" at the Scream Awards (2014), "Most Promising Youth Actor" at the Ping Awards (2014), and "Best Supporting Actor" at the Africa Magic Viewers' Choice Awards (2016). An alumnus of King's College, Lagos, and Babcock University, where he studied Mass Communication, Oworu is also passionate about youth empowerment and public service. In 2023, he ventured into politics, running for a seat in the Lagos State House of Assembly to represent Surulere 1 constituency under the Labour Party

Noray Nehita is an emerging Nigerian actress who made her screen debut in the 2025 drama Mother’s Love, directed by Omotola Jalade Ekeinde. With a passion for storytelling and a commitment to portraying nuanced female characters, she aims to contribute meaningfully to the evolving landscape of African cinema. Mother’s Love serves as a compelling introduction to her capabilities as an actress, and she is poised to take on more challenging roles in the future.

Ifeanyi Kalu is a multifaceted Nigerian actor, model and fashion designer. Hailing from Nwangele Local Government Area in Imo State, he pursued a degree in Computer Science at Lagos State University before transitioning into the entertainment industry. Lilian Afegbai is a Nigerian actress, film producer, and entrepreneur. She gained initial recognition as a housemate on Big Brother Africa in 2014, which served as a springboard into Nollywood. She has since appeared in numerous films and television series, including Tinsel, The Women, Moms at War and Hotel Labamba. In 2018, Afegbai made her production debut with the film Bound, which won the Africa Magic Viewers' Choice Award (AMVCA) for Best Indigenous Language (Igbo) . Beyond acting and producing, she is the founder of EEP Entertainment and launched a lingerie and gym wear line called "Lilly’s Secret" in 2019 . Afegbai is also known for her advocacy on women's rights and body positivity, using her platform to inspire and empower others.

Nosa Rex Okunzuwa, popularly known as Baba Rex, is a celebrated Nigerian actor, producer, and content creator renowned for his dynamic presence in Nollywood. He earned a degree in Mechanical Engineering from Ambrose Alli University before venturing into acting in 2010. His breakout role came with Gazza Treasure and he gained widespread recognition for his portrayal of Terwase in the hit TV series Jenifa’s Diary. Over the years, Nosa Rex has built an impressive filmography, featuring in notable films such as Lagos Real Fake Life, Omo Ghetto: The Saga, The Wildflower and A Tribe Called Judah. Beyond acting, he is the CEO of Big Things Development, a film production company and has ventured into fashion and automobile businesses
