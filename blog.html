THE REAL ME: Behind the Curtain of a Cinematic Family

8th Dec 2025

A Foundation Unshakeable: The Ekeinde Clan

Beyond the red carpets, the global film sets, and the mantle of awards, Omotola Jalade Ekeinde, MFR, has built a life as robust and respected as her career: that of a dedicated wife and mother. Her family life is not a footnote to her success; it is the powerful foundation that sustains her three-decade legacy. Omotola's journey with her husband, Captain Matthew Ekeinde, is one of Nollywood’s most celebrated partnerships. They share a unique bond, having been married in a symbolic ceremony mid-air—a fitting start for a union that has soared through the heights of international fame.

The Captain and the Queen

In a world where celebrity unions often face relentless scrutiny, the Ekeindes have anchored their marriage for over two decades. The secret, according to the actress, lies in a shared commitment and her husband’s supportive nature. Captain Ekeinde, an airline pilot, is described as having a calm personality and prioritizing peace over the spotlight, ensuring the home front remains a sanctuary from the relentless demands of Hollywood and Nollywood.

Omotola has explicitly credited their longevity to this shared foundation and mutual respect, noting the value of his simple-mindedness and lack of competition with her public profile.

The "Omosexy" Philosophy: Sacrifice None

Omotola and Captain Matthew Ekeinde are blessed with four children: Princess, Captain E (Matthew Jr.), Meraiah, and Michael.
The actress has consistently demonstrated that a woman does not have to choose between ambition and family. Her philosophy is clear: “Sacrifice none. If you design your life intentionally, you don’t have to lose yourself to succeed.” This commitment is visible in her approach to raising her children—a firm but loving parenting style—and in her early decision to introduce her family to the public eye via the groundbreaking reality show, Omotola: The Real Me.

Today, the Ekeinde children have grown into successful adults in their own right, with their mother’s dedication to structure and discipline having guided them into their respective fields. For Omotola, family is the ultimate project, proving that a life built on strong values can thrive even under the brightest lights of global stardom.
